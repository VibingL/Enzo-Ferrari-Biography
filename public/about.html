Enzo Ferrari

Enzo Ferrari, born on February 20, 1898, in Modena, Italy, was the founder of the iconic Italian sports car manufacturer Ferrari. He had a deep passion for racing from a young age. After serving in World War I, he became a test driver for a small car manufacturer. In 1929, he founded Scuderia Ferrari, a racing team that would later evolve into the Ferrari automobile company.

Ferrari's racing team achieved considerable success in the 1930s, but it was after World War II that Ferrari began producing road cars under his own name. The first Ferrari road car, the 125 S, was built in 1947.

Throughout his life, Ferrari remained heavily involved in motorsport, leading his racing team to numerous victories in Formula One and other racing series. He was known for his intense dedication to racing and his relentless pursuit of perfection.

Enzo Ferrari passed away on August 14, 1988, leaving behind a legacy as one of the most influential figures in the automotive industry. Today, Ferrari is synonymous with luxury, performance, and Italian design, and remains one of the most prestigious automotive brands in the world.
